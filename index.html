What is Phishing?

Phishing is the practice of tricking victims into revealing confidential data through scam emails, texts, or phone calls, and it's one of the biggest headaches for cybersecurity professionals.

In March 2024, a phishing attack targeted customers of the popular payment service, PayPal. The attackers sent emails claiming that the recipients' accounts were flagged for suspicious activity and required immediate verification. The emails contained a link to a fake PayPal login page designed to look identical to the real one. After users entered their credentials, the attackers accessed their accounts and performed unauthorized transactions. Over 100,000 users worldwide were affected, with millions of dollars in transactions stolen. PayPal issued a statement warning users to be cautious of unsolicited emails and emphasized the importance of verifying the sender’s email address and URLs before clicking any links.

How to Identify Phishing Scams

Protect yourself from online threats by following these cybersecurity tips to spot phishing attempts before they can cause harm.

  • Verify Sender Email Address: Always check the sender’s email carefully for small inconsistencies, such as a misspelling or odd domain.
  • Hover Over Links: Hover your mouse over links to preview their destination URL before clicking. Ensure the URL is from a trusted source.
  • Beware of Urgency or Threats: Phishing emails often use alarming language to pressure you into acting quickly. Legitimate companies won’t demand immediate actions.
  • Check for Grammar and Spelling Errors: Professional emails are usually free of mistakes. Phishing emails often contain typos, incorrect phrasing, and poor formatting.
  • Never Share Sensitive Information: Avoid sharing personal or financial information over email. Always contact the company directly through their official website.
  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication: Enabling multi-factor authentication (MFA) adds an extra layer of security, making it harder for attackers to gain access.
